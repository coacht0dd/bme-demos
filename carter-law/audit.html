Part 2

What the Evidence Means

Finding 01
Your personal injury practice is invisible — the page literally doesn't exist.

Anyone who searches, finds you, and clicks "Personal Injury" hits a dead 404 page. In a market where one signed case is worth five figures in fees, every dead click is a case that walks to Valdosta.

Finding 02
Google doesn't know what you do or where you do it.

No H1, a title tag of "Carter Law |", no meta description, and zero schema markup. When someone in Cook County searches "injury lawyer near me," Google guesses — and it guesses a billboard firm 25 miles away.

Finding 03
Your 116 Google reviews are working for Google, not for you.

You've earned a 4.4-star reputation. Not one of those reviews appears on your website — the place where an injured person decides whether to call you or keep scrolling.

Finding 04
You serve 17 counties but can only be found in one town.

There is no page for Valdosta, Tifton, Moultrie, Nashville, or the I-75 corridor. No page means no ranking — you're forfeiting every town except your own street address.

Finding 05
Your prospective clients can't find you — or your father's legacy.

Both attorney bios 404. Three generations of Carters practicing in Adel is the most powerful trust story in your market, and the website tells none of it.
